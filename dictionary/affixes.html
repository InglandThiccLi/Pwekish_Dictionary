Introduction

Pwekish employs a wide range of affixes in both word formation and the expression of abstract ideas. Affixes in Pwekish are either prefixes, which are added to the beginning of a word, or suffixes, which are attached to the end. They not only allows Pwekish to form numerous new words from a small set of roots, but also play an important role in Pwekish grammar. Some affixes provide spatial or temporal information to a sentence, while others modify a word's part of speech, or even alter the sentence structure. In a nutshell, affixes are central to Pwekish as it grants flexibility and extendability to the language.

Effect on Stress

Pwekish words are usually stressed on the second-last syllable. There are some exceptions, as indicated by an accent mark (◌́) on vowel letters. The addition of some affixes may shift the stress pattern of the word, while some have no effect on the stress patterns of words. We can classify the affixes into type S and type T.

Type S means the stress pattern of the resultant word remains the same as before after adding the affix. For example, the stress of "dip" is on itself. If we add "fisi-", a type S prefix, to the word and forming "fisidip", the stress is still on "dip".

Type T means the stress pattern of the resultant word is changed after adding the affix. For example, the stress of "fisidip" is on "dip". If we add "kut-", a type T prefix, to the word and forming "kutfisidip", the stress will change and fall on "fisi", as words are stressed on the second-last syllable in the case that no accent mark presents.
